Cemmentary for “YongZheng Dynasty”

After watching the TV series “YongZheng Dynasty”, based on my current state of mind, what i feel is the unpredictablity of the human heart.

The redicilousness of my process of returning to school

originally, i had communicated with the academic affairs office that the tuition remained from the previous semester would be used to pay for the tuition for the next semester. However, today they suddenly said that i need to pay the remaining balance. i don’t even know if the student loan i applied for would be processed in time. This kind of situation is really exhausting-i almost couldn’t handle it.

Moreover, if i don’t asked about the process of returning to school today, i might loss my student status. They never mentioned that i needed to apply for re-enrolling before January 8th.

This leadership team is completely foolish. i might be the first case of taking a break from school, but now it’s turned into a precedent,a lesson for others.

I often say, “You learn from your mistakes”,and that’s something to keep in mind for next time, but when it comes to this kind of experience where a single mistake can ruin everything, the next opportunity might not come. Some things can only be learned once, and failing can cost you your entire future. So, many times, you need to act with caution. This experience feels like life is telling me: I can make countless mistakes, but you only get one.

Right now, the most urgent thing is getting my re-enrollment application approved, so at least my student status will be secured, and the tuition issue can be dealt with later. In hindsight, when facing certain things, i really shouldn’t approached them with a youthful midset. Some things don’t allow for any error. These situations need to be carefully considered, and one should act with great caution. However, many times, the first mistake is too common, so sometimes going against the grain carries a very high risk. Only those who succeed are remembered in history beacuse of survivor bias, while those who fail are forgotten by everyone.

Overall, it’s important to keep a long-term perspective. Academics are crucial, and the money enrned after graduation can be used to pay it back.
